Crop Growing Instructions

How to grow Cotton?


1. Soil Preparation: Cotton grows well in black, loamy, or alluvial soil with good drainage. Plow the land 2–3 times and apply organic manure.

2. Sowing: Sow cotton seeds between April and May when the temperature is warm. Ensure row spacing of about 75–90 cm and 25–30 cm between plants.

3. Irrigation: Provide irrigation every 15–20 days. Avoid water stagnation. Reduce watering after flowering to encourage boll opening.

4. Pest & Disease Management: Use pheromone traps and organic pesticides to prevent bollworms and aphids. Crop rotation also helps.

5. Harvesting: Harvest cotton when bolls burst open and turn white. Pick in dry weather and store in a clean, dry place.

6. Common Pests: Bollworm, Aphids, Whiteflies

7. Artificial Nutrients: Nitrogen (Urea), Potassium Sulphate, Phosphorus (DAP)
How to grow Rice?


1. Soil & Climate: Rice grows best in clay or loamy soil with good water-holding capacity. It needs a tropical climate with high humidity.

2. Nursery Preparation: Sow seeds in a raised nursery bed. Prepare the field by puddling and leveling it for transplanting.

3. Transplanting: After 25–30 days, transplant seedlings into the main field with 15 cm spacing.

4. Water Management: Maintain 2–5 cm standing water in the field. Drain before harvest.

5. Harvesting: Harvest rice when grains are firm and golden. Dry thoroughly before storage.

6. Common Pests: Rice Stem Borer, Leaf Folder, Brown Planthopper

7. Artificial Nutrients: Urea, Super Phosphate, Muriate of Potash
How to grow Sugarcane?


1. Soil Preparation: Sugarcane needs well-drained loamy soil enriched with organic matter. Plow the field deeply and level it.

2. Planting: Use healthy 2–3 bud stem cuttings. Plant them in furrows at 75–100 cm row spacing. Time: February to March or September to October.

3. Irrigation: Regular irrigation is vital, especially during early growth and dry spells. Avoid waterlogging.

4. Fertilization: Apply compost, nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium fertilizers. Earth up after 2 months.

5. Harvesting: Harvest after 10–12 months when canes are mature, hard, and sugary. Cut close to the ground for high juice recovery.

6. Common Pests: Early Shoot Borer, Top Shoot Borer, White Grubs

7. Artificial Nutrients: Urea, Phosphate, Potash, Zinc Sulphate
How to grow Tomato?


1. Soil & Season: Tomato thrives in well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter. Sow in early summer or post-monsoon.

2. Seedling Preparation: Grow seedlings in nursery trays or seedbeds. Transplant after 25–30 days when 3–4 leaves appear.

3. Transplanting: Keep 60 cm between rows and 45 cm between plants. Water immediately after planting.

4. Care & Management: Stake the plants for support. Apply mulch and use drip irrigation. Remove weeds regularly.

5. Harvesting: Harvest tomatoes when fully red and firm. Avoid over-ripening on the plant for longer shelf life.

6. Common Pests: Fruit Borer, Whitefly, Thrips

7. Artificial Nutrients: Calcium Nitrate, NPK 19:19:19, Micronutrient mix
How to grow Maize?


1. Soil Requirements: Maize grows best in well-drained fertile loamy soil with good organic content. pH range: 6.0–7.5.

2. Sowing: Plant seeds in rows 60–75 cm apart, spacing 20–25 cm between seeds. Ideal sowing time: June–July or January–February.

3. Irrigation: Irrigate at key stages: germination, flowering, and grain filling. Avoid overwatering.

4. Fertilizer Use: Apply compost and nitrogen-rich fertilizer. Top-dress with urea during growth.

5. Harvesting: Harvest when husks dry and kernels harden. Dry the cobs properly before storing or shelling.

6. Common Pests: Fall Armyworm, Stem Borer, Corn Earworm

7. Artificial Nutrients: Urea, DAP, Potassium Nitrate
How to grow Onion?


1. Soil & Climate: Onion requires loose, well-drained sandy loam soil. Best grown in cool weather for bulb formation.

2. Nursery & Transplanting: Sow seeds in nursery beds and transplant after 6 weeks when 15 cm tall. Keep 10–15 cm between plants.

3. Irrigation: Provide light watering at regular intervals. Avoid water stagnation, especially near maturity.

4. Fertilization: Use compost, phosphorus, and potassium fertilizers. Top-dress with urea during bulb formation.

5. Harvesting: When tops fall and turn yellow, stop irrigation. Harvest after 7–10 days. Cure onions under shade and store in a dry, airy place.

6. Common Pests: Onion Thrips, Onion Maggot, Leafminers

7. Artificial Nutrients: DAP, MOP (Muriate of Potash), Sulphur
